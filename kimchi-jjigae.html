Kimchi Jjigae (Kimchi Stew)

Characteristics

Kimchi Jjigae is a classic Korean stew made with well-fermented kimchi, pork, and tofu. It's known for its deep, spicy, and savory flavors that come from the aged kimchi. As one of the most beloved comfort foods in Korea, its taste can vary widely depending on the additional ingredients. It's typically served hot with a side of rice, and its flavor deepens the longer it's simmered.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 head of sour kimchi (300g)
  • 200g of pork (shoulder or belly)
  • 1/2 block of tofu
  • 1/2 green onion
  • 3 cups of water or anchovy broth
  • Seasoning: 2 tbsp red pepper flakes (gochugaru), 1 tbsp minced garlic, 1 tbsp sugar (if kimchi is very sour), 1 tbsp soy sauce or salted shrimp

Recipe

  1. Cut the pork and kimchi into bite-sized pieces. Slice the green onion and tofu.
  2. In a heated pot with a little oil, stir-fry the pork. When the surface is cooked, add the kimchi and continue to stir-fry.
  3. Pour in the water or broth and add all the seasoning ingredients (red pepper flakes, minced garlic, sugar, soy sauce).
  4. Once the stew boils, reduce the heat to medium and simmer for at least 15 minutes to let the flavors meld.
  5. Finally, add the tofu and green onion, and cook for another 2-3 minutes before serving.
